    \subsection*{Ancient Greek: Reflexive Pronouns Contracted Forms by Crasis}
    \begin{itemize}[label={}, leftmargin=0]
        \item Kristoffer E.D.\ Paulsson (2026), Independent Scholar.
    \end{itemize}
    Smyth (1956:93, \S329 a) says ``For \grc{ἑαυτῶν}, etc., we find \grc{αὑτῶν}, \grc{αὑτοῖς}, \grc{-αῖς}, \grc{αὑτούς},
    \grc{-ά̄ς}. Distinguish \grc{αὑτοῦ} of himself from \grc{αὐτοῦ} (328).''

    The third-person reflexive \grc{ἑαυτός} undergoes regular Attic crasis, in which \grc{ε} is elided,
    yielding \grc{αὑτός}, with \textit{spiritus asper} (rough breathing) on the initial alpha in both singular and plural.
